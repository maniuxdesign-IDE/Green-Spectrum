# Green Spectrum Product Context Brief

## Role of This Brief

This brief is context only. It should inform future design and development work, but it should not be treated as permission to design pages, construct components, define navigation, or infer features beyond a specific future request.

Green Spectrum should be approached as a guided sustainability innovation system, not as a conventional sustainability website.

## Product Definition

Green Spectrum consists of two complementary products:

1. The Green Spectrum Website: a digital platform that guides individuals and organisations through sustainability challenges step by step.
2. The Green Spectrum Facilitation Method: a workshop and co-creation system using canvases, activities, collaborative tools, and facilitation methods.

The website and facilitation method can operate independently, but together they form a broader sustainability operating system.

## Core Purpose

Green Spectrum helps users:

- Understand sustainability in practical terms.
- Identify where to start.
- Choose which sustainability challenges to address.
- Select appropriate tools, methods, frameworks, and activities.
- Involve the right stakeholders.
- Move from awareness to implementation.
- Generate innovation, alignment, roadmaps, and measurable impact.

Its emphasis is navigation rather than information. The platform should help users understand when to use a tool, what problem it solves, and what comes next.

## Primary Users

Green Spectrum is primarily for people creating positive change inside organisations, including:

- Sustainability professionals
- Strategic designers
- Service designers
- Innovation consultants
- ESG teams
- Transformation teams
- Design engineers
- Product teams
- Innovation managers
- Chief Sustainability Officers
- Consultants
- Students learning sustainable innovation

Secondary users include SMEs, startups, public sector organisations, NGOs, universities, workshop facilitators, and internal innovation teams.

The experience should be accessible for beginners while still feeling rigorous to experienced practitioners.

## Product Goals

Green Spectrum aims to:

- Make sustainability easier to navigate.
- Reduce complexity.
- Democratise sustainability knowledge.
- Help organisations move beyond compliance.
- Support meaningful innovation.
- Increase collaboration.
- Bridge sustainability, business, and design.
- Create repeatable innovation processes.
- Become a recognised methodology for sustainable innovation.

## Content Scope

The website may eventually include:

- The complete Green Spectrum methodology
- Interactive journeys
- Learning resources
- Decision trees
- Navigation tools
- Assessment tools
- Framework recommendations
- Templates
- Workshop guides
- Canvases
- Innovation activities
- Case studies
- Practical exercises
- Progress tracking
- AI-assisted guidance
- Resource libraries
- Implementation roadmaps
- Links between process stages

These should only be designed or built when specifically requested in future prompts.

## Experience Principles

The user should never feel overwhelmed. Every interaction should help answer:

1. Where am I?
2. Why am I here?
3. What should I do next?

Green Spectrum should feel like an experienced sustainability coach guiding users through uncertainty one step at a time.

Every page or activity should move users forward with an appropriate next step, such as continuing the journey, downloading a canvas, running a workshop, inviting colleagues, creating a project, completing another assessment, exploring tools, booking facilitation, generating a roadmap, saving progress, exporting outputs, or tracking maturity.

## Website and Workshop Relationship

The website teaches and guides by explaining why something matters, how a tool works, and when to use it.

The workshops create action by helping teams discuss, create, prioritise, align, prototype, and make decisions.

Workshop outputs should be able to feed back into the website, and website projects should be able to transition into facilitated workshops when collaboration is needed.

## Codex Guardrails

Before building anything:

- Do not generate page layouts.
- Do not create components.
- Do not assume navigation structure.
- Do not infer unrequested features.
- Treat this as product vision and context only.
- Wait for future prompts to define individual pages, features, interactions, flows, and technical implementation.
