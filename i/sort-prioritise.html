Section 01

Capture the problem signals worth sorting.

Add or edit the problems you want to compare. These can come from the journey map, ESG reports, stakeholder interviews, operational data, or group discussion.

Section 02

Read the priority landscape.

The page automatically arranges problems into a heat map, the Green Spectrum, and the Cynefin framework so the team can see where effort, impact, maturity, and complexity are pulling in different directions.

Suggested priority

Choose the three to five problems to take forward.

The suggested ranking favours high impact, strategic importance, urgency, and lower effort. Select the problems that are most relevant to the organisation and workshop brief.

Section 03

Use the decision tree cascade for each selected problem.

Each tree combines the Green Spectrum maturity path with Cynefin action logic: checklist, expert analysis, prototype, or stabilise.

Canvas elements for Page 5

Decide what is worth taking forward.

  • Specific problem statement.
  • Who and what is impacted.
  • Business or sustainability goal.
  • Where it occurs in services, systems, or the value chain.
  • Cynefin complexity and Green Spectrum ambition.
  • Realistic response options: process, product, behaviour, or system.
  • Most relevant key opportunity.

Canvas elements for Page 6

Design the intervention and prototype plan.

  • Project name and chosen solution.
  • Immediate, mid-term, and long-term action timelines.
  • People involved and approval needs.
  • Resources needed and partnerships required.
  • Hypothesis for test.
  • Success measures and evidence of progress.
  • Simple next step for the prototype or strategy sprint.
