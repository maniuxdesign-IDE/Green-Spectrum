                Questions about Impact Journey Mapping

                How many stages should the journey contain?

                Use enough stages to reveal meaningful differences without making the map unmanageable. Three to eight primary stages is usually appropriate.

                What if our process is not linear?

                Use stages as an organising structure, then add feedback loops, dependencies and repeated flows to show how the system behaves.

                Should I map the whole organisation?

                Only if that scope is useful and manageable. A product, service, business unit, value chain or specific challenge often produces clearer insights.

                What if I do not have all the data?

                Record the gap, its importance and where evidence may be found. Unknowns are a legitimate output.

                Should I include positive impacts?

                Yes. The map should show strengths, value creation and existing capabilities as well as harm, risk and weakness.

                How is this different from a customer journey map?

                The Impact Journey Map includes activities, decisions, stakeholders, data, environmental impacts, social impacts, governance, business outcomes and system relationships.

                Should I choose solutions on this page?

                No. Record opportunity areas, but delay final solution selection until the system has been structured and priorities have been compared.

                Can a problem appear in more than one stage?

                Yes. Repeated problems often indicate a system-level issue and should be linked across all relevant stages.
