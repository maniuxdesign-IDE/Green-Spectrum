Why Green Spectrum exists

Sustainability leaders rarely lack information. They lack a clear path through it.

Chief Sustainability Officers are expected to manage reporting, regulation, operational change, stakeholder expectations, risk and long-term transformation at the same time. The challenge is deciding where to begin, what matters most and what to do next.

Too many competing priorities

Climate, nature, people, governance, operations, regulation and commercial pressures all compete for attention.

Disconnected evidence

Reports, operational data, expert knowledge and stakeholder experience often remain separated across teams and systems.

Unclear decision logic

Organisations may identify many possible initiatives without knowing which one deserves time, ownership or investment first.

Pressure to act with uncertainty

Leaders must make progress before every relationship, consequence or impact can be fully understood.

Green Spectrum turns these disconnected pressures into one structured journey.

What it is

Not another sustainability framework. A connected way to use the ones that already exist.

Green Spectrum combines systems thinking, sustainability practice, strategic design, decision-making and experimentation into one connected process.

Understand

Make the system visible

Explore the organisation through business, human and planetary perspectives before defining solutions.

Navigate

Structure complexity and choose focus

Organise challenge signals by maturity, complexity, impact, effort, urgency, evidence and strategic relevance.

Act

Turn priorities into learning

Evaluate options, select interventions, design experiments and improve decisions through evidence.

One process. Six connected stages.

From uncertainty to informed action.

Each stage builds on what came before. Green Spectrum helps users understand before prioritising, and test before scaling.

  1. 01

    Explore Three Empathies

    What is happening across the business, human and planetary system?

    Build a rounded understanding of the challenge before moving into solutions.

    Inputs and outputs

    Inputs: organisational knowledge, stakeholder experience, environmental evidence, existing reports.

    Outputs: maturity signals, tensions, behaviour patterns, evidence gaps, initial problem signals.

  2. 02

    Map Problem Landscape

    Where do impacts, decisions and dependencies occur?

    Map the journey, stakeholders, activities, impacts, relationships, unknowns and feedback loops.

    Inputs and outputs

    Inputs: problem signals, journey stages, stakeholder knowledge, operational processes.

    Outputs: system map, impact journey, hotspots, feedback loops, missing evidence.

  3. 03

    Structure Spectrum and Complexity

    What kind of challenge is this?

    Classify challenges by sustainability ambition and complexity before choosing a response.

    Inputs and outputs

    Inputs: issue signals, evidence confidence, maturity level, cause-and-effect understanding.

    Outputs: Green Spectrum profile, complexity profile, challenge clusters, structured portfolio.

  4. 04

    Prioritise Select Focus

    Which challenges should receive attention first?

    Compare impact, effort, urgency, strategic relevance, influence, evidence and learning value.

    Inputs and outputs

    Inputs: structured challenges, priorities, resources, risk, influence.

    Outputs: ranked portfolio, quick wins, strategic bets and selected priorities.

  5. 05

    Decide and Experiment Decision Pathway

    What is the right next move?

    Evaluate whether to proceed, experiment, pause, archive or return to discovery, then design a measurable learning activity.

    Inputs and outputs

    Inputs: selected priority, evidence, capability, risk, strategic alignment, testability.

    Outputs: pathway, owner, hypothesis, prototype, success measures, experiment plan.

  6. 06

    Reflect and Evolve Continuous Learning

    What did the organisation learn?

    Review results, capture evidence and feed learning back into future decisions.

    Inputs and outputs

    Inputs: results, observations, metrics, feedback, unexpected consequences.

    Outputs: learning record, revised priorities, next cycle and improved capability.

Need clarity before starting?

Frequently asked questions

Is Green Spectrum an ESG reporting tool?

No. Green Spectrum supports the thinking and decision-making that often needs to happen before reporting, implementation and investment. It can use reporting data as evidence, but its primary purpose is strategic navigation and organisational change.

Do I need complete data before starting?

No. Green Spectrum records evidence confidence and gaps so users can distinguish what is known, assumed or still unclear.

Can I use Green Spectrum by myself?

Yes. Solo Mode is designed for sustainability leaders conducting research, planning, problem framing or early-stage strategy work independently.

When should I involve other people?

Involve others when the challenge depends on knowledge, experience, authority or ownership that sits beyond one role.

When should I use the facilitation tools?

Use the facilitation tools when the process would benefit from shared interpretation, stakeholder input, collective ownership or live systems mapping.

Do I need to use every canvas?

No. The Green Spectrum process determines which tool is useful. The tools support the work but should not become additional administrative burden.

How long does the process take?

A focused journey may be completed across several short sessions. Larger organisational challenges may require repeated cycles.

Can I go back to an earlier stage?

Yes. Green Spectrum is iterative. New evidence may require users to revisit exploration, mapping, structuring or prioritisation.

Can I download my work?

Yes. Individual outputs and consolidated reports should be exportable. The Resources and Downloads page contains the reusable Green Spectrum tools and reference materials.

Is Green Spectrum only for large organisations?

No. The process should adapt to different sectors, organisation sizes and sustainability maturity levels.

Does Green Spectrum replace a sustainability consultant?

No. It supports structured thinking and decision-making. Some challenges will still require specialist expertise, facilitation, technical analysis or external assurance.

How does the platform use my information?

Information entered into the platform is used to connect context, evidence, insights, priorities, decisions and experiments within the active workspace.
