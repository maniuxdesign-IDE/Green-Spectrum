Questions about interventions and experiments

What is the difference between an intervention and an experiment?

An intervention is the intended change. An experiment is a structured way to test an assumption about that intervention before larger implementation.

Do I need three pathways?

Three pathways are recommended because they create a balanced portfolio, but one well-developed pathway is better than three weak ones.

What is backcasting?

Backcasting starts with a desired future outcome and works backwards to identify the conditions, capabilities, decisions and actions required to reach it.

Where did the larger roadmap work go?

Roadmaps, learning loops and complete journey reporting are kept as placeholders for later. The active MVP task is the prototype experiment card.

How small should an experiment be?

It should be the smallest credible activity that reduces an important uncertainty without creating unacceptable risk.

What if the problem is Complex?

Use safe-to-fail experiments, monitor patterns and adapt. Avoid assuming one intervention will create a predictable result.

Should failure end the pathway?

Not necessarily. A well-designed experiment can generate valuable learning even when the hypothesis is not supported.

Can I return to an earlier Green Spectrum stage?

Yes. New evidence may require a return to Explore, Impact Journey Mapping or Prioritisation.
