Questions about sorting and prioritising

Does the highest score automatically become the top priority?

No. Scores support judgement but do not replace it. Strategic balance, risk, values, readiness and stakeholder consequences may justify a different decision.

What is the difference between Green Spectrum classification and maturity?

Organisational maturity describes the organisation’s current position. Problem classification describes the level of sustainability response implied by a particular challenge.

What is the difference between a difficult problem and a complex problem?

A difficult problem may still have an analysable cause and effect. A complex problem involves interacting actors, feedback loops and outcomes that emerge over time.

Can one problem belong to more than one Cynefin domain?

Yes. A broad problem may contain Clear, Complicated and Complex elements. Break it into sub-problems where that improves decision-making.

Why does evidence confidence matter?

A problem may appear urgent or important but still be poorly understood. Low confidence often means the next action should be research or experimentation.

Why should I choose only three to five problems?

A small portfolio creates focus, ownership and realistic resource allocation. Other problems remain recorded and can be revisited later.

Should I always prioritise low-effort problems?

No. Low-effort actions can build momentum, but high-impact strategic problems may still deserve attention even when they require more capability or investment.

When should I return to Explore or Impact Journey Mapping?

Return when the problem remains unclear, evidence is weak, important stakeholders are missing or system relationships are not sufficiently understood.
